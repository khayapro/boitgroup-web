Who are we?

Boit Talent Management was founded in 2011, and has had 5 years experience working with South African Talents. Boit TM is a full-service booking agency representing celebrities, speakers, live entertainers, sport stars, models, casting characters, promoters, photographers and makeup artists. Our diversity within the industry is what sets us apart from our competitors. We hold ourselves accountable to both our Talents and Clients by honoring our commitments, providing results and striving for an unsurpassed level of service. We assist with all aspects of negotiating, contracting and oversee the execution of your booking requirement.

Percy Mphaho

Motivational Speaker

Percy is one of the few self-motivated entrepreneurs in South Africa, all his achievements are all based on hard work and giving back to rural communities. Percy was born and bred in a dusty remote area in Limpopo Polokwane from a village called Londsdale, he grew up looking after his parents’ cattle while working on his book whilst doing his house chores.
Mr Percy Mphaho realised after passing matric that there were more opportunities to look forward to outside the rural boundaries other than being a Doctor, a career his parents wished for him to take. Because of lack of career guidance at high school level, he was one of those who did not apply for higher education right after matric. “But God had a purpose” he says.
Percy is a founder of Vision 2015, an NGO established a decade ago in year 2000 after graduating from Vaal Triangle Technikon. The Mission of Vision2015 is to bridge Digital Divide in South Africa because of the imbalance education system in South Africa. To date with the help of Telkom, Eskom, Heinman Publishers, Alcatel SA, SABC, Vision 2015 can boast that 90% of the schools in South Africa have their signature (Vision2015) because of the computers at their schools.
In 2010, Percy opened Mphaho Holding Company that currently has four companies namely, Presley Info Tech (IT Company), Neocomit (IT retail stores), PM Minerals (Minerals and Energy), Lim Taba Tours (Transport and Accommodation). In 2012, PM minerals secured 4000 hectares of land in Waterburg Lephalale to do exploration of Coal mining to date. Percy was selected as a Deputy President of Lephalale Chamber and was selected as a member of Ward Council committee in the ANC. He also was a manager of Caster Semenya, 800 meter South African Sports Athletic Champion, during the time of her glory. Percy is also a motivational public speaker and mostly focuses on Technology to create youth development- especially in rural communities.

Lebo Gunguluza

Motivational Speaker | Business Speaker | Key Note Address | Business Mentor
The Breakthrough Entrepreneur

Mr Gunguluza is considered one of South Africa’s most celebrated media entrepreneurs, a strategist and a motivational speaker. At the age of 27, he became one of South Africa's youngest self-made black millionaires, without any funding or tenders from government, and over the years, he built a multimillion-rand empire where he had to overcome tremendous odds on his entrepreneurial journey.

He is one of the key founders of the South African Black Entrepreneurs Forum to address issue facing black entrepreneurs in South Africa. Gunguluza adapted the title of "'The Ultimate Breakthrough Entrepreneur" and was awarded with the "Africa Heritage Youth Entrepreneur Award" five years later. Events, publishing and hospitality expert Lebo Gunguluza knows what it’s like to succeed – and then fail – at business ideas. Gunguluza worked as marketing manager for Metro FM before being headhunted by advertising agency Herdbuoys and then taking his first steps into entrepreneurship. Mr Gunguluza’s businesses – including Gunguluza Entertainment and Corporate Fusion – have seen Gunguluza travel a challenging path before finally scoring big with Gunguluza Enterprises & Media which bow boasts 12 print publications, stakes in numerous hotels and a car-hire business. He was recently honoured by the Millionaires Club with the Leadership Award in May 2010, and received the Bethesda Business Award for his contribution to the development of Entrepreneurship in South Africa.

Mr. Gunguluza is also a founder, Group Chairman of the Gem (Gunguluza Enterprises and Media) Group established in 2002, an integrated media and hospitality group with interests in companies that serve in the media, communications, hospitality, and IT spheres. Mr.Gunguluza is the President of SABEF, has an extensive experience in communication and media having consulted as National Director of Media Communications for membership-driven organizations such as Nafcoc and the Institute for Local Government Management. Lebo Gugunguluza is currently one of the judges in a renowned show called The Dragon’s Den, where he and the other judges will be investing as well as mentoring the selected aspiring entrepreneurs.

Gugu Zulu

Sports Speaker | Car Racer | Presenter | Actor
The Fastest brother in Africa

Gugulethu Zulu, affectionately known as "the fastest brother in Africa", is known for his confidence, ambition and passion for cars. He is recognised as one of the finest rally and race car drivers in the country. Gugu was also a presenter of popular SABC motoring show Car Torque, and in 2008, he showed off his dance moves as a celebrity contestant in the reality show Strictly Come Dancing. In 2005 he acted in a film called Lord of War directed by Andrew Nicol.

Gugu began his racing career in 1999, after graduating from the Isondo Racing Academy. In 2000 he shocked the racing world by winning the Vodacom Isondo Sports 2000 national championship, winning 13 out of 16 races with two second places. In 2001, racing legend Sarel van der Merwe took him under his wing, when he was given second seat in the inaugural Sasol Steam Team. That year he won the Guinness Achievement Award, as well as the Wesbank and Goodyear Star of Tomorrow awards. By 2002 he was setting lap times as fast as his mentor, Sarel van der Merwe.

In 2003 he was offered a drive in the rallying world, scoring high points in several rallies to bring him within one point of the championship leader. In 2004 he had a taste of overseas racing, competing against the likes of Mario Andretti’s grandson Marco, in the Skip Barber Dodge Racing Series. He joined the presentation team of Car Torque on Sunday 17 July, 2005. In August 2007 he was appointed as the commercial manager for A1 Team South Africa, which competes in (and hosts a race for) the A1GP, "The World Cup of Motorsport".

The year 2000 is when Mandoza’s career really took off with the mammoth hit album “Nkalakata” selling over 10 times platinum and winning numerous awards stated.

Mandoza became the first black South African Artist to crossover to all nations in the country and became a major role model to his fans. Mandoza has recently released his 9th solo album end of 2008 called “Ingwenya” which has already seen the singles blazing up the charts. 2 DVD’s have also been released, “Mandoza Live at Sun City” and “Mandoza Double Pack” also a release of a cosmetic range consisting of perfume, deodorant, lip balm, sun screen etc breaking ground in 2009 called Mandoza – “Col-lab-o-ra-tion”. Mandoza has wooed crowds of over 40 000+ people per event being the headline act in Africa predominantly in the Sadic regions like Lesotho, Botswana, Mozambique, Swaziland, Zambia etc! Malik

Musician

For Malik, music has always been the food of love. When times looked awry during his childhood, it was always music that brought the comfort and joy he sought. It was music that provided the escape he needed. Music became his life and so began the journey to pursue a musical career. Being the only member of his family to have been bitten by the music bug, Malik didn’t have it easy. His family didn’t understand his unswerving devotion to music. “They were so protective. They feared that I would pursue a career in music, which frightened them because they always believed that there was something better for me to go after,” says Malik.

From a tender age Malik had attracted audience attention due to his captivating voice. He sang in a church choir and was always the envy of everyone because he was a communicative singer and performer. After finishing matric, his family encouraged him to take up a “respectable” career path. He enrolled to study Bio Technology at the Tshwane University of Technology. After completion of his studies, he was fortunate to land a job at a Coca Cola lab. “It just didn’t feel right; I didn’t like it one bit and so I quit. My mom freaked out, but I had to follow my heart,” he recalls.

Following that, Malik began his pursuit of a career in music. He met up with a friend Kyllex Faku, with whom he worked on the production side of things. He played around on his guitar writing music that he didn’t know would later be published. He worked with a number of people, honing his skills and learning the art of music. As fate would have it, he was later introduced to some of the industry giants, for whom and with whom he would later produce award-winning songs. Some of the notable giants include the likes of Ringo Madlingozi and Zola 7.
